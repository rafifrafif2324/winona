The Weight of How Much You're Loved

funny how time moves
you were a girl once,
bare hands, bright eyes, dreams still in their wrappers.
now you stand here soft but certain,
the kind of grown that doesn’t just show in age,
but in grace.

you’ve walked through fire quiet.
you carried storms in your chest and still held space for sunlight.
you earned this day
this celebration of who you’ve become,
and everything still unfolding.

outside, they’ll talk about your beauty
the way your skin glows,
the way your body moves like a song you don’t even realize you’re singing.
but it’s the inside of you that makes everything shine louder.
the way you care, the way you love,
the way you keep choosing light even when darkness felt easier.

you’ve earned your name in full bloom.
you’ve earned joy without guilt.
you’ve earned softness without apology.

and i just hope today, you feel it
not just the candles, the messages, the gifts
but the weight of how much you’re loved.
how rare you are.
how much you’ve grown.

you’re not just beautiful.
you’re meaningful.
you’re matter.

pink matter.

and you’ve only just begun.
