Gravity Dressed in Beauty
there’s something sacred in your face. like light caught in still water, like the first time you see a painting that feels like it’s staring back. the way your eyes hold warmth it’s not just the color, it’s the way they look at the world, like everything’s worth loving. your lips soft like velvet, shaped like poetry before it’s written. i swear, when you speak, the air listens. your skin glows like dusk not just golden, but tender, alive. like sunlight lives under it. your hands… delicate but strong like they were made to hold softness, to create, to comfort. even the way you move feels like music. grace without trying, art without effort. i don’t just notice you i’m pulled to you. like gravity dressed in beauty. like something my soul remembered before my body did. you don’t even try. and that’s what wrecks me.
